Official blueprint and vision for ITM AIR Lab (Artificial Intelligence Research Lab) — an open-source, student-driven research laboratory focused on foundational research in Large Language Models (LLMs) and Computer Vision Models (CVMs).

Badges
- Status: Student‑driven • Open source
- Roadmap: 0–12 months
- License: MIT

## Mission
- Open-source research and tools.
- Focus on fundamental AI development with emphasis on LLMs and CVMs.
- Promote transparency, reproducibility, and high-quality academic output.

## Core Philosophy
- All research and releases are open-source.
- Emphasis on reproducible experiments, clear documentation, and ethical research practices.
- Foster a mentorship pipeline for junior researchers.

## Organization
Three core departments:
- Research Department: paper writing, experimental design, model architecture.
- Data Department: dataset creation, cleaning, annotation, curation.
- Development Department: training, fine-tuning, deployment, evaluation.

Each department will have:
- Department lead (student)
- Contributors (students, interns)
- Faculty/industry mentors (as available)
  
Responsibilities: long-term strategy, funding and grants, partnerships, and ethical oversight.

## Transparency & Outreach
- Maintain a daily-updated website that includes:
  - Research progress and publications
  - Datasets and model releases
  - Lab news and technical articles
- Host regular seminars, workshops, and hackathons.


## How to Contribute
- Read CONTRIBUTING.md for the process.
- Open issues for proposed projects or contributions.
- Follow the branch/PR naming and review workflow in CONTRIBUTING.md.
